Student Profile:

Travis Williamson

What are we, if nothing but the sum of our experiences? I feel like I have led a fairly interesting life; always willing to try the new, be uncomfortable, take a chance. My experiences range from working on a sport fishing boat in Alaska to owning a private tutoring business in South Korea.

Not knowing what to study, I thought business administration was proabably a reasonable field. I majored in Finance, but in my third year, my artistic itch had to be scratched, so I took 2 quarters of art classes to re-evaluate. It was fun, but ultimately decided that a business degree would serve me better than one in art.

After gratduating from the University of Oregon in '94', I immediately eschewed the idea of a "real" job and got a job bartending on a cruiseship. Always looking for the next thing to keep my interest, I then moved on to restaurant managment and then to merchant processing.

My life changed one day when I read an advertisement to teach ESL in South Korea. I interviewed and three weeks later I landed at Gimpo Int'l Airport. ot called back on the way home from the interview to be told I had the job

I have spent the last twentyone years building a successful life and small education business in Korea. That's a long time in one place, doing the same thing.

I decided that this point in my life would be as good as any to try something new; to reinvent myself, so we loaded up the truck and we moved to... The USA.

Planning to do things I just didn't have time to do while running a business, the first step in this life evolution life has taken me down a road less, nay, never, travelled by me before; coding!
